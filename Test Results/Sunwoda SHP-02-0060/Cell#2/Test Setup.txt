This was a single charge cycle test at 11C, to invalidate DonutLab's claim that no NMC lithium cell could handle 11C charging from empty to 80% without active cooling.  Specifically, their claim was (cringe emojis and all):
"First of all, not a single NMC cell would perform like that during full 11C charge without active cooling and reaching 89 celsius.🤭 Happy to cover all the test costs and a little bonus to anyone who successfully does it with VTT or other trustworthy research lab and proves otherwise. ⚡️"

Test results:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=unQrgIvD5Xg

The cell in this video accepted 5.848 Ah in 319 seconds (5.3 minutes)(11.3C).

After this charge test completed, the cell rested for one hour, and was then discharged at 1C (6 A) back down to the starting voltage (3.0 volts).
During this discharge, the cell sourced 5.79 Ah.  So round trip coulomb efficiency was 99.2% (5.8/5.848Ah).  Note that actual energy efficiency (Wh) was lower, due to the difference in voltage between charge and discharge.
